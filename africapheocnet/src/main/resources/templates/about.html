ABOUT Africa PHEOC-NET

PHEOCs save lives

The Africa PHEOC Network was established in November 2015 by the WHO Regional Office for Africa in coordination with the WHO Health Emergencies Programme. The Network is a key regional platform for collaboration, communication, and the exchange of information for ultimately improving the quality and efficiency of public health emergency management.

A Journey Through Africa PHEOC-NET Story

our mission

The Network seeks to build and sustain national capacity to manage public health emergencies at all levels of the health system. The Network will act as a platform for enhancing partnerships and the synergy of national, regional and global efforts to support countries in the WHO African Region.

Crafting Expertise: Africa PHEOC-Net's Development of Regional Training and Technical Materials

In 2018, Africa PHEOC-Net developed essential training materials and technical documents, including the Handbook for PHEOC and legal framework guidelines, for member states.

A comprehensive review of these resources took place in Brazzaville from October 22nd to 26th, 2018.

Empowering the Pioneer Cohort: Africa PHEOC-Net's Inaugural ToT Workshop for PHEOC Experts

Africa PHEOC-Net organized a transformative nine-day workshop, bringing together a regional team of experts at Public Health Emergency Operations Centres (PHEOCs). Their goal? To establish new centers and bolster the capacity of WHO Member States in coordinating, preparing for, and responding to public health emergencies.

Empowering Africa's Response: Africa PHEOC-Net Leads Regional Functional Simulation Exercise

Africa PHEOC-Net spearheads a regional simulation exercise on December 4th-5th, 2019, engaging 17 African countries.

Held in Brazzaville at the WHO African Region headquarters, the exercise simulates a response to a communicable disease outbreak in Eastern Africa, eventually revealed to be Ebola.

Strengthening Regional Capacity: 2nd Training Workshop on PHEOC

The 2nd Training of Trainers Workshop on Public Health Emergency Operations Centre (PHEOC) convened with the aim of bolstering countries' capacity in coordinating and responding to Public Health Emergencies (PHEs).

The workshop focused on equipping Regional Trainers to conduct cascade trainings for national PHEOC and Incident Management System (IMS) staff, emphasizing PHEOC operations and management.

Launch of Five-Year Strategic Plan to Strengthen Public Health Emergency Operations Centers in Africa

In a collaborative effort, the Africa CDC, WHO, member states, the UK Health Security Agency, and partners unveiled a transformative five-year plan during the third International Conference on Public Health in Africa.

Aimed at strengthening PHEOCs across Africa and the Eastern Mediterranean, the plan seeks to enhance health preparedness and response capabilities in at least 50 African countries.
